During my master’s thesis, I investigated the host specificity of one basal fungi, called an aphelid, on several different microalgae. I drew pictures of the representative strains (above) as part of my master’s thesis defense. One of my main results was that the aphelid was an obligate parasite on the strain UTEX 393 and could act as a parasite towards other related strains, but was most virulent to the microalgal strains that were closely related to UTEX 393. In this case, that meant being in the same species as UTEX 393 but a different strain or being in the same genus.
